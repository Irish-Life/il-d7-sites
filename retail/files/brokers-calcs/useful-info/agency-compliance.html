Agency & compliance

FATCA (Foreign Account Tax Compliance Act)


Due to new United States legislation, from 1st July 2014, new policyholders will be asked to confirm if they are a U.S. citizen or U.S. tax resident. There will be a new question on our ePOS system and paper application forms for investment, savings and unit-linked, whole of life products. Please read the Q&A document for full details of these new requirements.



Please note that applications received by Irish Life before 1st July and have not yet issued by 1st July must also comply with this legislation. As pre-1ST July application forms (ePOS or paper) have not asked the FATCA question, customers will have to complete and sign a supplementary form (click here) before their policy can be issued.



Phase 2 has now been implemented (December 2014) in respect of new policies where the proposers are not individuals e.g. company-proposed new business. Additional information will be required in such cases. Please read this document for more details

From Monday 13th January 2014, Irish Life will be introducing some small changes in relation to SEPA


(Single Euro Payments Area) requirements. Please see our Questions & Answers document which will give you a background to SEPA and explain the impact any changes have on our business. Click here to view or download a copy of the SEPA Q&A. The SEPA-compliant direct debit mandate will be required for new business submitted from 13th January 2014.Click here to download a copy of the SEPA-Compliant DDM.

Life products and the question of residency


Irish Life is not licensed to sell, market or solicit its products outside the Republic of Ireland. Please see our rules for accepting life assurance, savings, investment and pension plans in terms of residency.

 
  • Residency and Irish Life products

If one of your customers who is resident in the Republic of Ireland, but is temporarily out of the country, wishes to transact life insurance business, the “Declaration of Residence in the Republic of Ireland” must be completed and signed.

 
  • Declaration of residence

Distance sales


Are you writing business with a customer who you will never meet face to face during the course of arranging the sale i.e. on a distance basis?

The Distance Marketing Directive 2004 requires that where a distance sale takes place, you must provide your client with the following documentation prior to completion of the sale:

 
  • Your firm’s terms of business letter
  • Irish Life product brochure and generic CIN – available on this site
  • Irish Life terms and conditions booklet – available on this site
  • Certain other information contained in schedule 1 the regulations; Irish Life schedule 1 document

Irish Life must be satisfied that when a sale has taken place on a distance basis, you have provided this information to your client. Therefore, we would ask you to submit confirmation of this at time of making the application e.g. providing a copy of the cover letter you sent to the client when providing the documentation.
