Damian Fadden

Director of Sales and Client Services

Damian is Director – Sales & Client Services with Irish Life Corporate Business. In this role, he has overall responsibility for sales, client services and product management for Irish Life’s corporate pension and insurance clients.

Damian holds an MBA from the UCD Michael Smurfit School of Business, and is an actuary by profession. He is a former Council member of the Society of Actuaries in Ireland and currently sits on the Society’s Pensions Committee.

Prior to his current role, Damian held a number of roles at Irish Life across the marketing, finance and actuarial areas.

David Harney

Managing Director, Irish Life Corporate Business

David joined Irish Life in 1986. He is a Fellow of the Society of Actuaries in Ireland and a Fellow of the Institute of Actuaries. He also holds a Masters in Financial Mathematics from DCU.

He has worked in Corporate Business since 1998 and has held roles of Director of Finance, Director of Marketing and Director of Sales. He was appointed Managing Director Irish Life Corporate Business in 2008.

Irish Life Corporate Business is the leading provider of Group Defined Contribution pensions schemes, Group Life Assurance and Disability schemes and post retirement annuities in Ireland. It has market share of approximately 40% in each of its product areas.

Eoin Keating

Head of DC portfolio Management

Eoin joined Irish Life Investment Managers (ILIM) in 2007 and is responsible for the pension and investment relationship with large institutional defined contribution clients. Prior to joining ILIM Eoin worked for almost 8 years in Ulster Bank on client facing lending teams in the Property Finance and Corporate Banking divisions.

In addition he worked on the Ulster Bank Debt Capital Markets team helping to arrange and execute a range of capital and funding transactions.

He graduated from Dublin City University with a Bachelor of Business Studies degree in which he specialised in Finance and he also holds the M.Sc. in Investment & Treasury from Dublin City University and holds the Q.F.A.

Gerry Hassett

Managing Director - Irish Life Financial Services

Irish Life Financial Services (ILFS) is the retail arm of the Irish Life Group, offering a range of investment, pension & risk products to individuals & small business owners. It distributes through Financial Brokers, Bank branches and its own direct salesforce (including a telesales unit). Gerry has been MD of ILFS since 2007, and worked in Irish Life since 1987. He is a former Chairman of Insurance Ireland, and currently Chair of its Pensions Policy Working Group. He holds a Masters in Marketing from University College Dublin.

Patrick Burke

Managing Director, Irish Life Investment Managers

Patrick was appointed Managing Director of Irish Life Investment Managers (ILIM) in 2013, having joined ILIM in 2006 as Director of Investment Development with the responsibility for the strategic development of investment solutions to the institutional market.

Prior to joining ILIM, Patrick was a Director and professional trustee at Irish Pensions Trust Limited having previously served as Corporate Lawyer to Mercer. Patrick is past-Chair of Pensions Europe, past Chair of the Irish Association of Pension Funds and has previously served as Chairman of the Irish Trustee Forum. A Solicitor by profession, he is also an Associate of the Irish Taxation Institute and an Associate of the Irish Institute of Pension Managers. He holds a primary Degree in Commerce from University College Dublin.

Matt Cooper

Journalist, Author and Presenter

Matt Cooper is the presenter of ‘The Last Word’ on Today FM, one of the nation’s most popular radio shows. In over twenty years as a reporter (Business & Finance, Sunday Business Post, Irish Independent) and editor (Sunday Tribune), and now as a broadcaster and columnist (Irish Daily Mail), Matt Cooper has enjoyed access to the most significant people in Irish politics and business, putting him in an unrivaled position to assess and interpret the actions of Ireland’s most powerful figures.

He was awarded Business Journalist of the Year in 1992 and 1999 and also National Journalist of the Year in 1993 and 2001. He is a graduate of Dublin City University. He is the author of two bestselling books, Who Really Runs Ireland? (2009) and How Ireland Really Went Bust (2011). He also narrated the acclaimed documentary ‘Dennis Rodman’s Big Bang in Pyongyang’ (2015) after he travelled with the basketballer to North Korea in 2014. He is a television sports host with TV3 and presented the station’s 2015 Rugby World Cup coverage.

From Cork, he lives in Dublin with his wife and their five children.
