Securing Your Clients Legacy

Helping your clients protect their assets from Inheritance Tax when they pass them on to their beneficiaries is a vital component of the financial advice process. However, helping them to appreciate that this is something they need to consider will require careful guidance from you on what can be a difficult subject to talk about.

Supporting you on your journey with your client

Many people, it is probably fair to say, are not comfortable considering their own death, or what to do with their assets when they die. Many of your clients may not have discussed it with their families, and may be reluctant to do so. It is also probably fair to say that unless they are prepared to make a plan for what happens after their death, they are unlikely to consider what to do about the tax bill on their estate.

The first step, therefore, has to be the will. If they do not have a will, there is potential for confusion, and even conflict, upon their death. Considering what to put into their will and making the definite plans for what is to happen to their assets, will start the process of full and proper planning and may help your client get over any reluctance they may have to talk about their death.

After going through the process of drafting their will, your clients may well feel more ready to engage with the conversation about the tax bill on their estate.

With proper planning, the Capital Acquisitions Tax (‘CAT’) bill due on an estate can be reduced as much as possible.

After that, the most financially efficient way to pay the bill may be a Section 72 plan, and once they know what their plan is, your clients can then consider whether this is an option they wish to consider. The bill will fall due on their estate after their deaths, and this is where you as the Adviser can potentially provide the solution.
