FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

My client has an existing whole of life protection plan that he no longer needs for family protection. Can he have that endorsed as a ‘special’ Section 72 approved policy ?

No.
The plan has to be taken out specifically from day 1 as a ‘special’ plan to pay inheritance tax. What the Revenue Guidance Notes state is that the plan has to be taken out expressly to pay Inheritance Tax.

How soon after death does the inheritance tax bill have to be paid ?

In accordance with Section 46 of the Capital Acquisitions Tax Consolidation Act 2003, a person who receives a gift or an inheritance with a valuation date in a year ending 31 August is required to pay gift or inheritance tax and file a tax return by the following 31 October.

So, where the valuation date of an inheritance is between 1st September 2017 and 31 August 2018, the tax bill has to be paid by 31st October 2018.

What happens if the life assured dies and the contract is only in force 6 years ?

The relief will NOT be lost as long as the plan was set up for a term of at least 8 years from the outset.

If the sum assured paid out from the policy is higher than the actual tax bill what happens ?

Any excess sum assured over the inheritance tax liability is paid to the estate and is divided in line with the life assured’s Will. This excess amount will be a taxable inheritance for these beneficiaries.
